      Bengaluru gearing up for Khelo India University Games

      Bengaluru: The Government of Karnataka, along with the Ministry of Youth Affairs and Sports, is all geared up to host Khelo India University Games 2021, the biggest national sporting extravaganza post pandemic in association with Jain University. The second edition of the Khelo India University games was supposed to be held in the year 2021 but was postponed to 2022 due to the Covid -19. The event, which aims to create a platform for youth to showcase their sporting excellence and provides universities from across India a chance to showcase their supremacy outside the education curriculum, is slated to begin on April 24 and conclude on May 3. The sporting extravaganza will be inaugurated by Hon’ble Vice President Sri Venkaiah Naidu. Over 4,500 participants representing around 190 universities will vie for top honours in 20 different sports including indigenous sports like Mallakhamba and yogasana being introduced for the first time in Khelo India University Games 2021.
